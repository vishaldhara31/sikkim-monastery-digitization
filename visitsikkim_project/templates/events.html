The Legend of Yoksum

In 1642, three learned Lamas from Tibet met at Yoksum and consecrated Phuntsog Namgyal as the first Chogyal (King) of Sikkim. This event birthed the spiritual identity of the land, intertwining the monarchy with Buddhist Dharma.

Bumchu

A unique vessel of holy water is opened to predict the state's prosperity. If the water is clear and the vessel full, it signifies a year of peace and abundance.

Mountain Worship AUGUST

Pang Lhabsol

A festival unique to Sikkim, paying homage to Mount Khangchendzonga as the guardian deity. It features the warrior dance performed by monks in spectacular costumes.

Traditional oboe-like instrument used to summon deities during rituals.

Nature's Festival

Cultural life in Sikkim is deeply tied to the land. Every spring, the state celebrates the blooming of over 400 species of Orchids and the majestic Rhododendrons.

Tastes of Tradition

Sel Roti

A ring-shaped sweet fried bread made from rice flour, essential for festivals like Tihar.

Spiritual Etiquette

Honoring the sanctity of the monastic space.

Remove footwear before entering any shrine or prayer hall.

Make small donations or light a butter lamp as a gesture of respect.

Maintain silence and avoid using mobile phones inside temples.
