Sikkim's Cultural Calendar

Discover the vibrant festivals and sacred events that shape the year in Sikkim. Plan your visit to coincide with these unique cultural celebrations.

January / February

Maghey Sankranti

A major Hindu festival marking the start of a holy month. Jorethang in South Sikkim is famous for its large fairs (melas).

Losar

The Tibetan New Year, celebrated with immense fervor in monasteries like Rumtek and Pemayangtse with prayers and cultural dances.

May / June

Saga Dawa

The most sacred Buddhist festival, commemorating the birth, enlightenment, and passing of Lord Buddha. Grand processions with holy scriptures are held in Gangtok.

International Flower Festival

Held in Gangtok, this festival showcases Sikkim's rich diversity of flora, including hundreds of species of orchids and rhododendrons.

August / September

Tendong Lho Rum Faat

A festival of the Lepcha community, celebrating the Tendong Hill which is believed to have saved them from a great flood. Treks to the hill are common.

Pang Lhabsol

A unique Sikkimese festival that worships Mount Kanchenjunga as a guardian deity. The warrior dance is a major highlight at Tsuklakhang Palace.

October / November

Diwali

The Hindu festival of lights is celebrated with great enthusiasm throughout the state, especially in towns like Gangtok and Namchi.

December

Losoong / Namsoong

The Sikkimese New Year, marking the end of the harvest season. It's a time for joyous celebrations, archery contests, and vibrant masked dances in monasteries.

Kagyed Dance

A festival preceding Losoong, where monks in monasteries perform intricate masked dances to ward off evil spirits and welcome the new year.
